Food-Grade / FDA Compliance Statement

Mandatory for any product contacting food or water consumed by animals.

RequiredSilicone BodyHard Cap
  • Silicone must comply with FDA 21 CFR §177.2600 (rubber articles intended for repeated use) or §177.1210 (closure compositions).
  • Hard plastic cap must comply with FDA 21 CFR §177.1520 (polyolefin resins, e.g. PP) or applicable section for Tritan™ or ABS.
  • Correct on-pack language: "Made with food-grade, FDA-compliant silicone" — never "FDA approved."
  • Retain supplier material certification letters (COA/SDS) referencing the 21 CFR section for regulatory audit trail.
+
🚫

BPA-Free Claim

Highly expected by US consumers — defensible if substantiated.

RecommendedHard Plastic Cap
  • Silicone is inherently BPA-free — safe to claim.
  • For the hard plastic cap: BPA is specific to polycarbonate. If the cap is PP, Tritan™, or similar, it is BPA-free by composition — confirm with supplier material data sheet.
  • Per FTC Green Guides (16 CFR Part 260), claims must be specific and substantiated. Do not claim "BPA-free" without supplier certification or third-party test report.
  • Suggested on-pack copy: "BPA-Free Cap — Tested & Certified" with a third-party lab name where possible.
⚠️If you cannot obtain a third-party BPA test certificate for the plastic cap, limit the claim to the silicone body only, or remove the claim to avoid FTC exposure.
+
🐾

Pet-Safe / Non-Toxic

Common consumer expectation for pet products — must be defensible.

RecommendedSubstantiation Required
  • No single US regulatory body "approves" pet product materials beyond FDA food-contact rules.
  • "Non-toxic" and "pet-safe" claims are governed by FTC Act Section 5 (deceptive advertising) — they must be substantiated.
  • Commission a third-party migration/toxicology test (e.g. via SGS, Intertek, or Bureau Veritas) to support the claim.
  • ASTM F963 (toy safety standard) is sometimes cited for indirect reassurance but is not directly applicable — avoid referencing it on-pack unless specifically scoped.
+
🏭

Country of Origin

Mandatory under US Customs & CBP regulations.

MandatoryProduct & Packaging
  • Must appear on the product itself or permanently affixed label: "Made in China" (or country where substantially transformed).
  • Governed by 19 U.S.C. §1304 and CBP regulations. Must be legible, permanent, and conspicuous.
  • If assembled in China from components, China remains the COO. Verify with your import/export counsel if any component substitutions occur.
+
📋

Manufacturer / Importer Information

Traceability and consumer contact — required for CPSC compliance.

MandatoryCPSC
  • Include importer/distributor name and US address on packaging per CPSC regulations.
  • Include a customer service contact (email or phone) for product safety communications.
  • Per CPSC Section 14(a), consumer products must carry a tracking label (production batch/date/location). A moulded-in date code on the base satisfies this for the silicone body.
+
⚠️

Prop 65 Warning (California)

Required if selling in California and product contains listed substances.

California OnlyConditional
  • California Proposition 65 (Safe Drinking Water & Toxic Enforcement Act) requires warnings if products expose consumers to chemicals on the state's list at levels above safe harbor thresholds.
  • Common triggers for silicone/plastic products: lead, phthalates, DEHP, DBP. Conduct XRF screening and migration testing.
  • If clean test results are obtained, no Prop 65 warning needed — retain test records. If uncertain, affix a warning label to avoid civil litigation exposure.
  • Minimum compliant warning (short form): "WARNING: This product can expose you to chemicals including [name], which is known to the State of California to cause [cancer/reproductive harm]. For more information go to www.P65Warnings.ca.gov."
Recommendation: The symbols below are industry-standard and consumer-trusted. Recommended symbols are those with the highest impact for a pet water bottle product. Optional symbols add value but require additional testing or certification cost.
ℹ️Symbols without a certified programme (e.g. a "dishwasher safe" symbol you design yourself) are permissible but must be accurately substantiated. Where certified third-party marks exist, they carry more weight with consumers and retailers.

US Patent Marking — 35 U.S.C. §287

Core marking obligation for patented products sold in the US.

Mandatory if Patented
  • Physical marking (traditional): Affix the word "Patent" or abbreviation "Pat." followed by the patent number(s) to the product or packaging. E.g. "Pat. US10,123,456".
  • Virtual marking (since AIA 2011): Mark with "Patent" or "Pat." followed by a URL directing consumers to a page listing applicable patents. E.g. "Pat. agilian.com/patents". This is the preferred method for manufactured products as it avoids retooling when patents are added or expire.
  • Marking must be applied to as many units as reasonably practicable. Failure to mark limits damages recovery to post-notice infringement only.
  • False marking (35 U.S.C. §292): Marking a product as patented when it is not (or after a patent has expired) is a federal offence. Do not use "Patent Pending" on a product once a patent has granted or been abandoned.
+
⏳

"Patent Pending" Marking

Used when a patent application has been filed but not yet granted.

During Application Phase
  • Permissible once a provisional or non-provisional application is filed with the USPTO.
  • Provides notice to potential infringers and may deter copying, though it confers no legal enforcement rights until the patent grants.
  • Must be removed or updated to the granted patent number promptly after grant. Continued use of "Patent Pending" after grant or abandonment is deceptive.
  • For moulded silicone products, consider moulding-in a placeholder slot or printing on packaging to allow cost-effective updates without retooling.
+
🌍

International Patent Marking Considerations

If selling outside the US, different marking rules apply.

EU / UK / AUAdvisory
  • UK / EU: No statutory obligation to mark, but marking is commercially prudent and aids enforcement. Format: "EP [number]" or "GB [number]".
  • Australia: No marking requirement, but marking supports damages claims. Format: "AU Pat. [number]".
  • Virtual marking (a URL) is the most flexible approach for international products — a single marking links to a jurisdiction-sorted patent page.
  • Recommended: Maintain a patents.agilian.com (or customer brand equivalent) landing page with all applicable patents listed by market.
ℹ️Agilian NPD recommends using virtual patent marking (a URL on the product base or packaging) as standard practice across all OEM programmes — it eliminates retooling risk when the patent portfolio changes and satisfies US, EU, and AU requirements with a single mark.
Regulatory context: The FTC Green Guides (16 CFR Part 260) govern all environmental marketing claims in the US. The FTC may investigate and prosecute deceptive green claims. Claims must be specific, substantiated, and not create a misleading impression. Vague terms like "eco-friendly," "green," or "sustainable" without qualification are high-risk.

🟢 Silicone Longevity / Durability Defensible

Silicone has outstanding durability versus single-use plastics — it resists UV, ozone, and extreme temperatures. A claim such as "Built to last — reduces single-use plastic waste" is defensible and specific. Substantiation: material longevity data from the silicone supplier (e.g. >10,000 flex cycles, temperature range). This is the strongest and lowest-risk sustainability claim available for this product.

🟢 Reusability / Replaces Single-Use Defensible

A reusable pet water bottle directly displaces single-use plastic bottles. Claims like "Reusable — replaces hundreds of single-use bottles" are accepted and encouraged by the FTC when specific and accurate. Quantify the claim conservatively (e.g. "replaces 300+ single-use bottles over its lifetime" if backed by a reasonable usage model).

🟡 Silicone Recyclability Caution

Silicone is technically recyclable (devulcanisation process) but is not accepted in standard municipal recycling streams in most US cities. A general "recyclable" claim would be deceptive under FTC Green Guides unless you specify conditions. A defensible alternative: "Recyclable through specialist silicone recycling programmes — contact us for details." Consider partnering with TerraCycle or a silicone recycler to make this real and documentable.

→ Silicone recycling programme information (external)

🟡 Hard Plastic Cap — Recycled Content Verify with Supplier

If the cap resin contains post-consumer recycled (PCR) content, this is a strong and specific claim: e.g. "Cap made with 30% recycled plastic." Requires supplier certification of recycled content percentage. If virgin resin only, this claim is not available — do not make it.

🔴 "Eco-Friendly" / "Sustainable" (unqualified) High Risk — Avoid

Broad, unqualified claims like "eco-friendly," "sustainable," "good for the planet," or "green" are the primary target of FTC Green Guides enforcement. Without specific, substantiated environmental benefit data attached to each claim, these terms expose the brand and importer to FTC investigation and retailer delisting. Do not use these terms unqualified. Instead, replace with specific claims (see above).
